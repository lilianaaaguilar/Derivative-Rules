Quotient Rule

If you have one function divided by another, you’ll need the quotient rule—it helps keep your fractions clean and organized.

You can use this rule when you are finding the derivative of one function divided by another.

How to do it:

(f ⁄ g)’ = (f′g - fg′) ⁄ g2 (low d’ high minus high d’ low, over low squared)

Examples:

f(x) = x2 ⁄ (x + 1) : f’(x) = ((2x)(x + 1) - (x2)(1)) ⁄ (x + 1)2

f(x) = sinx ⁄ x : f’(x) = ((cosx)(x) - (sinx)(1)) ⁄ x2
