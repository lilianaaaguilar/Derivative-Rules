Chain Rule

The chain rule may be confusing at first but so helpful once figured out.

You can use this rule when you are deriving a function inside of another function. It can also be used with the power rule.

How to do it:

(outer ⋅ inner)’ = (derivative of outer)(derivative of inner)

Examples:

f(x) = (3x + 1)5 : f’(x) = 5(3x + 1)4(3)

f(x) = sin(x2) : f’(x) = cos(x2)(2x)

Chain Rule → use when one function is inside another.

  • Find the derivative of: f(x) = (3x2 + 1)5
  • A. 5(3x2 + 1)4 · 3x
  • B. 6x(3x2 + 1)5
  • C. 5(3x2 + 1)4 · 6x
    • Explanation: f'(x) = 5(3x2 + 1)4 · 6x
  • D. 15x(3x2 + 1)4
  • Find the derivative of: f(x) = sin(x2)
  • A. cos(x2)
  • B. -sin(x2) · 2x
  • C. cos(x2) · 2x
    • Explanation: Chain rule → derivative of sin(u) is cos(u) · u'
  • D. sin(x2) · 2x
  • Find the derivative of: f(x) = e5x3
  • A. e5x3 · 3x2
  • B. 15x2ex3
  • C. e5x3 · 15x2
    • Explanation: Chain rule on eu with u = 5x3
  • D. 5x2e5x3
