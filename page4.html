Chain Rule

The chain rule may be confusing at first but so helpful once figured out.

You can use this rule when you are deriving a function inside of another function. It can also be used with the power rule.

How to do it:

(outer ⋅ inner)’ = (derivative of outer)(derivative of inner)

Examples:

f(x) = (3x + 1)5 : f’(x) = 5(3x + 1)4(3)

f(x) = sin(x2) : f’(x) = cos(x2)(2x)

Chain Rule → use when one function is inside another.
