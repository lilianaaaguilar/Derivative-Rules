Product Rule

Sometimes functions are multiplied together, and we can’t just multiply their derivatives. That’s when the product rule comes in handy!

You can use this rule when you are finding the derivative of functions being multiplied together.

How to do it:

(f · g)’ = f′g + fg′ (first times derivative of second PLUS second times derivative of first)

Examples:

f(x) = x2(sinx) : f’(x) = (2x)(sinx) + (x2)(cosx)

f(x) = (x2 + 3)(x - 2) : f’(x) = (2x)(x - 2) + (x2 + 3)(1)

Product Rule → use when multiplying functions.
